UCL-GOSH Data Interchange for Sensors Classifications

Requirements

When trying to do a project, determining the needs of users is undoubtedly the most significant step. In this part, we actually summarised the roles of different users, and then classified demands according to MoSCoW list; in the follow-up, we continuously communicate with our client in meetings to finialise the MoSCoW requirements.

Research

In order to get familiar with what we will work with at the actual developing part, our client wanted us to do some researches and share our findings. Basically, it covers different types of sensors, types of sensors used in the industrial infusion pumps and Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) standards.

UI Design

We covered a lot of important content for the Human Computer Interaction (HCI) component of the module. It helped us, developers, to understand how to build interactive, effective, efficient user-centered design for the NHS Clinicians and for the parents that will be using our application. We will conduct additional interviews and apply the knowledge gained from lectures into practice by creating Personas, coming up with Scenarios, and building Prototypes.
